General Dentistry

A smile is a curve that sets everything straight

This is the primary department where routine dental check-ups, cleanings, and general dental treatments are performed. General dentists handle various dental procedures such as fillings, extractions, and oral health evaluations.

Oral Surgery

In the hands of an expert oral surgeon, the power of healing and transformation resides.

The oral surgery department focuses on surgical procedures involving the teeth, jaws, and surrounding structures. Oral surgeons perform complex extractions, dental implants, bone grafts, and corrective jaw surgeries.

Endodontics

In the hands of an endodontist, a tooth's life is extended, and a smile is preserved

Endodontics is the specialty that deals with the diagnosis and treatment of the dental pulp and tissues inside the tooth. Endodontists perform root canal treatments to save damaged or infected teeth.

Dental Hygiene

A healthy smile begins with excellent dental hygiene

Dental hygienists work in this department, providing preventive dental care such as cleanings, scaling, and polishing to maintain oral health.

Prosthodontics

In the hands of a prosthodontist, broken smiles find new beginnings.

Prosthodontics is the dental specialty that focuses on restoring and replacing missing teeth with prosthetic devices, such as crowns, bridges, dentures, and dental implants.

Periodontics

A healthy mouth starts with strong and resilient gums.

Periodontics involves the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of gum diseases and conditions affecting the supporting structures of the teeth. Periodontists manage periodontal surgeries and various gum treatments.

Maxillofacial Surgery

Where science meets art, maxillofacial surgery reshapes lives.

Maxillofacial surgery, also known as oral and maxillofacial surgery (OMS), is a specialized branch of medicine that deals with the diagnosis, treatment, and surgery of conditions affecting the mouth, jaws, face, and associated structures. It's a unique specialty that combines aspects of dentistry and medicine to address a wide range of complex issues involving the oral and facial regions.

Canal Surgery

Root canal is just like a small dragon guarding your tooth.

Root canal surgery, also called endodontic surgery, is a dental procedure performed to save a tooth that is severely infected or damaged.

Oral Pathology

In the realm of oral health, oral pathology unveils the hidden truths of diseases.

Oral Pathology is a specialized branch of dentistry that deals with the study, diagnosis, and management of diseases and conditions that affect the oral and maxillofacial regions. It focuses on understanding the nature, causes, and manifestations of various oral diseases, ranging from common conditions to rare and complex disorders.

Orthodontics

In the hands of an orthodontist, crooked smiles find their perfect alignment.

Orthodontics is a specialized branch of dentistry that focuses on the diagnosis, prevention, and treatment of dental and facial irregularities, particularly those related to the alignment and positioning of teeth and jaws.

Frequently Asked Questions

Dental questions refer to inquiries or concerns that individuals have regarding their oral health, dental procedures, treatments, and general dental care. Patients often seek answers to these questions from dental professionals to gain clarity and make informed decisions about their dental health.

  • Are your dental procedures painful?

    Our team prioritizes patient comfort. We use modern techniques and anesthesia to minimize discomfort during dental procedures. Our dentists ensure you are informed and relaxed throughout your treatment.

  • How often should I have a dental check-up?

    For most patients, a dental check-up every six months is recommended. However, your dentist may suggest more frequent visits based on your dental health needs.

  • What options are available for replacing missing teeth?

    We provide various tooth replacement options, such as dental implants, bridges, and dentures. Your dentist will help you choose the best solution based on your specific needs.

  • What should I do in a dental emergency?

    If you experience a dental emergency, such as severe toothache or a knocked-out tooth, contact our dental hospital immediately. We prioritize emergency cases and will provide prompt care to alleviate your pain.

  • What should I bring to my first visit?

    Please bring your identification, insurance information, and any previous dental records if you have them. It helps our dentists understand your dental history better.
