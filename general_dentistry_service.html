General Dentistry Service

General dentistry services encompass a wide range of dental care procedures aimed at promoting oral health, preventing dental issues, and treating common dental problems. Here are some of the key general dentistry services:


1. Dental Examinations: Regular dental check-ups are essential for maintaining good oral health. Dentists perform comprehensive examinations to assess the overall health of your teeth, gums, and mouth. They may also use X-rays to detect any hidden dental problems.


2. Dental Cleanings: Dental cleanings, also known as prophylaxis or scaling, involve the removal of plaque, tartar, and stains from the teeth. This helps prevent gum disease and tooth decay.


3. Fluoride Treatments: Fluoride is applied to the teeth to strengthen the enamel and make them more resistant to decay.


4. Dental Fillings: When cavities (dental caries) are detected, dentists remove the decayed portion of the tooth and fill the area with materials like amalgam or composite resin to restore the tooth's function and appearance.


5. Dental Sealants: Dental sealants are thin, protective coatings applied to the chewing surfaces of molars and premolars to prevent cavities in these vulnerable areas.


6. Root Canal Therapy: When a tooth becomes infected or severely damaged, a root canal procedure may be necessary to remove the infected pulp, clean the inside of the tooth, and seal it to save the tooth.


7. Extractions: Dentists may perform tooth extractions when a tooth is severely damaged, impacted, or causing crowding issues.


8. Crowns: Dental crowns, also known as caps, are used to cover and protect a damaged or weakened tooth, restoring its shape, size, and strength.


9. Bridges: Dental bridges are used to replace missing teeth by anchoring artificial teeth (pontics) to adjacent natural teeth or dental implants.


10. Dentures: Dentures are removable appliances used to replace missing teeth and surrounding tissues.


11. Teeth Whitening: Professional teeth whitening procedures can effectively remove stains and discolorations, brightening your smile.


12. Oral Health Education: Dentists provide valuable education on proper oral hygiene practices, including brushing, flossing, and maintaining a healthy diet.


13. Gum Disease Treatment: Dentists can diagnose and treat various stages of gum disease (gingivitis and periodontitis) to prevent further damage to the gums and supporting structures.


14. Mouthguards: Custom-made mouthguards can protect teeth and jaws during sports activities or bruxism (teeth grinding) while sleeping.


These are just some of the general dentistry services available. Dentists play a crucial role in helping patients maintain optimal oral health and address dental issues promptly to prevent more significant problems in the future. If you have specific concerns or dental needs, it's best to consult a dentist for personalized advice and treatment.
